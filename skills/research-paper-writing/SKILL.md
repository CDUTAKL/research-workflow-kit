# Research Paper Writing

Use this skill to produce clear, evidence-backed academic prose. It supports Chinese graduation theses and English research manuscripts. The writing should make the research problem, method, evidence, and contribution easy to evaluate.

## Core Rules

- Write from evidence: do not create results, citations, or claims that are not supported.
- Put the main point early in paragraphs and sections.
- Use concrete claims over vague importance statements.
- Keep contribution, method, and result claims distinct.
- Preserve the user's meaning when rewriting, but strengthen logic, flow, and academic tone.
- Mark missing citations or evidence explicitly.

## Workflow

Read `references/workflow.md` for section templates, paragraph patterns, Nature-derived polishing checks, and revision checklists. Read `references/source-map.md` for source provenance.

1. Identify the target section, language, audience, and available evidence.
2. Determine the section's job in the paper.
3. Draft or rewrite with a clear claim-evidence structure.
4. Check each paragraph for topic sentence, evidence, transition, and overclaiming.
5. For final polish, apply section-aware academic style checks: paper type, section job, reader question, tense, hedging, sentence length, citation integrity, and overclaim risk.
6. Return text plus unresolved evidence/citation gaps.

## Output Contract

For drafting, include:

- revised or drafted section
- rationale for major structure choices
- missing citations or evidence
- optional shorter/stronger alternatives when useful
- final-polish notes when Nature-derived style checks are applied

For thesis-wide writing, prefer this order unless the user provides a template:

1. method
2. experiments
3. result analysis
4. related work
5. introduction
6. abstract
7. conclusion

Do not overwrite a user's document unless they explicitly ask for file edits.
